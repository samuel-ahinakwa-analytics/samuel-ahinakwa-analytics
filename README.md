## Portfolio Projects

### Project 1 — AfriTech Electronics
**Customer Trust and Revenue Risk Analysis**

73,586 customer interactions · 5 platforms · NPS −46.23 · $37.2M revenue at risk

A consumer electronics brand was losing customer trust. The assumption was product recalls. The data showed something different — recalled and non-recalled products had a 0.2 percentage point difference in negative sentiment. Statistically zero. The damage was behavioural. The real problem: a poisoned pipeline where new customers became hostile VIPs over time, and 1,410 VIP customers at the Point of No Return — actively being targeted by competitors while their complaints remained unresolved.

**Tools:** Power BI · PostgreSQL · Python · Excel  
**Key finding:** $1,076,419 actively transitioning to competitors today  
**Deliverables:** 5 Power BI dashboards · Comprehensive consulting report · McKinsey-style presentation

### Project 2 — VendBridge (NexaLink)
**Post-Launch Performance and Monetisation Leak Analysis**

920 rows · 92 days · 5 regions · 2 devices · 1,266,726 non-converting active users

A digital marketplace platform launched across five global regions. Installs grew every month. Users returned daily. But nearly half of all daily active users browsed without buying — a monetisation leak of 1,266,726 user-days producing no transaction. The analysis identified LATAM iOS as the critical failure point: a crash rate of 220.14 per 1,000 DAU — 12 times the US rate — collapsing conversion from 53.41% in June to 37.48% in August.

**Tools:** Power BI · Tableau · PostgreSQL · SQL  
**Key finding:** 26,596 transactions recoverable in 90 days through one engineering fix  
**Deliverables:** 5 Power BI dashboards · Board strategy report · Analytical glossary

### Project 3 — Streamline Logistics Solutions
**Operational Performance and Capacity Analysis**

1,500 delivery records · 5 routes · 96.87% late delivery rate · Backlog +48% in 7 days

A logistics company was experiencing a capacity crisis. The assumption was that slower deliveries were driving customer dissatisfaction. The data showed the opposite — customers with longer delivery times reported higher satisfaction. The real driver was communication gaps. The structural bottleneck was Route 1 at 160.8 minutes versus a 144.7 minute benchmark, combined with driver workload imbalances of up to 56% over target.

**Tools:** Excel · Power BI  
**Key finding:** Communication gaps — not speed — were driving dissatisfaction  
**Deliverables:** KPI framework · Route performance analysis · Capacity recommendations
